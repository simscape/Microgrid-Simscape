Open Battery Energy Storage System Subsystem

The battery energy storage system (BESS) contains a battery pack and a DC-AC converter. You can pre-parameterize the Battery (Table-based) block using the available parts. For example, to build a battery pack with a specific kWh, choose any cell of your interest from the parts catalogue and multiply the open-circuit voltage array with a constant to get the desired output voltage and multiply the capacity with another constant to get the desired capacity. Battery (Table-based) block block is connected to the point of common coupling (PCC) through a DC-AC converter. The BESS subsystem also contains a power limiter module which limits the battery SOC during the charging and discharging phases. You can specify the maximum SOC and minimum SOC of the BESS from the subsystem mask.
In this example, the BESS is comprisess a number of battery cells connected in series and parallel to get the desired energy from the battery pack. A controlled current source connects in series with the battery such that a desired current is drawn from the battery pack to get the desired power from the BESS. This combination of battery pack and current source connect to the AC-DC Converter (Three-Phase) block, which in turn connects to the grid. You can modify the BESS subsystem in the Battery.slx model

Configure BESS parameters

This example parameterizes the Battery (Table-Based) block with a 40.7 Ah cell. The parameters of the block, including the number of cells in series and parallel, are scaled accordingly. These are the parameters you can specify in the BESS subsystem:
Minimum Battery SOC - Minimum operating SOC of the battery
Maximum Battery SOC - Maximum operating SOC of the battery
Energy Rating (kWh) - Energy rating of the BESS
Capacity Rating (kWh) - Capacity rating of the BESS
Nominal voltage (V) - Nominal operating voltage of the BESS
Frequency (Hz) - Frequency of the grid to which the BESS is connected
Winding Ratio - Winding ratio of the transformer which connects the BESS to the network

Open Solar Subsystem

You can pre-parameterize the Solar Cell block by using the available parts. For example, to build a solar plant with a 100 kW , choose any cell of your interest from the parts catalogue and multiply the open-circuit voltage array with a constant value to get the desired output voltage. Then multiply the number of cells in parallel with another constant value to get the desired output power. You can determine the maximum power point tracking (MPPT) voltage by using the SolarMPPTTestHarness.slx model. In the test harness, choose the same part chosen in the TechnoEconomicMicrogrid.slx model and run the SolarMPPTVoltage.m script. This script runs test harness multiple times for different solar irradiance values and generates the MPPT voltage. The software saves the MPPT voltage in the solarMPPT.mat file. The TechnoEconomicMicrogrid.slx file uses the data in this MAT file to obtain the MPPT voltage at different irradiance values. You can specify the maximum irradiance using the subsystem mask. In this example, the Solar cell block connects to a AC-DC Converter (Three-Phase) block. The converter connects to the PCC. The voltage reference to the DC-AC converter comes from a 2D Lookup Table block that contains the MPPT voltage data with respect to solar irradiance. You can modify the Solar subsystem using the Solar.slx model.

Open Wind Subsystem

The Wind subsystem comprises a wind turbine and a controlled current source. You can calibrate the rating of the wind farm by changing the radius of the wind turbine and choosing the required number of turbines. This subsystem contains two circuits. The mechanical circuit comprises the wind turbine and an ideal speed source. The electrical circuit comprises a controlled current source that connects to the PCC. The mechanical circuit provides the power output of the wind farm depending on the wind speed. The electrical circuit uses this power and feeds the current to the system. You can specify the maximum wind speed in m/s in the subsystem mask. You can modify the Wind subsystem using the Wind.slx model.

Configure Wind Subsystem Parameters

This example parameterizes the Wind Turbine (Mechanical) block with a 500kW turbine. The software scales all the parameters such as the number of cells in series and parallel. These are the
parameters you can configure for the Wind subsystem:

Rating (kW) - Rating of the wind farm
Turbine output (kW) - Rated power of each wind turbine in the wind farm
Frequency (Hz) - Frequency of the grid to which the wind farm is connected
Rotor speed (rpm) - Rotor speed values of the MPPT lookup table
Wind Speed - Wind speed values of the MPPT lookup table
Winding Ratio - Winding ratio of the transformer that connects the wind farm to the network

Open Generator Subsystem

The Generator subsystem comprises a synchronous machine, prime mover, and an exciter. The Prime mover subsystem consists of an ideal torque source controlled by the desired load on the generator. The Exciter subsystem contains a DC voltage source that connects to the field circuit of the generator. You can specify the generator rating, rated frequency, rated voltage and number of pole pairs using the subsystem mask. You can modify the Generator subsystem using the Generator.slx model.

Configure Parameters for Generator

These are the parameters you can configure for the Generator subsystem:
Rating (kW) - Rating of the generator
Rated Voltage (V) - Rated voltage of the generator
Frequency (Hz) - Frequency of the grid to which the generator is connected
Number of pole pairs - Pole pairs of the generator
Winding Ratio - Winding ratio of the transformer that connects the generator to the network

Open Load Subsystem

The Load subsystem comprises two controllable load blocks. One block acts as a noncritical load. The other block acts as a critical load. During any contingency, noncritical loads can be shedded while the critical loads can continue to operate.

Configure Load Subsystem Parameters

Winding Ratio, which is the winding ratio of the transformer that connects the load to the network, is the only parameter that you can configure for the Load subsystem.

Operator Control Room

To operate the circuit breaker of any subsystem, use the controls available in the operator control room subsystem.

Microgrid Controller

The Microgrid controller subsystem comprises the Supervisory Control and the Breaker Control subsystems. The supervisory control subsystem includes the energy management system (EMS) of the microgrid.

Energy Management System

In this example, the EMS logic enables when there is any loss of any source due to any unforseen reasons. This EMS logic dispatches the power among the available assets such that the load can have an uninterrupeted supply of power. This example implements two types of EMS:
  • Rule-based EMS
  • Optimization-based EMS
This example implements the rule-based EMS using Stateflow. In this approach, predefined rules dispatch the controllable assets. In the optimization-based EMS, the software runs the optimization algorithm during the simulation and suggests the optimal dispatch of the controllable assets.
